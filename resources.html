If you are suffering from a mental illness, it's really important to get the help you need and deserve. Although this website is a great start, please use the following information for reliable and quality help.







MENTAL ILLNESSES

According to MedlinePlus, mental disorders are 'conditions that affect your thinking, feeling, mood, or behavior.' About 1 in 5 adults, or 20%, experience mental illnesses in a given year. This same percentage of teens experience a mental health condition between the ages of 13-18. The prevalence of mental illness in our society has only emphasized the need for mental health awareness.

Anxiety

The following information belongs to the National Alliance on Mental Illness. Please visit this link to get more information.

Anxiety disorder occurs when intense feelings of distress or nervousness begin to interfere with normal everyday activites. Here are a few types of anxiety disorders:

General Anxiety Disorder: "Chronic, exaggerated worry" that interferes with concentration in common activites.

Social Anxiety Disorder: Irrational fear of embarrassment when it comes to social situations, generally leading to difficulty in conversations and isolation.

Panic Disorder: Characterized by instense feelings of terror that result in palpitations, chest pain, or shortness of breath.

Phobias: Characterized by severe reactions to irrational fears.

18.1% of the population suffers from anxiety in a given year

Treatment: If your anxiety begins to interfere with your everyday life, there are treatment options. In addition to psychotherapy, there are medication options, such as antidepressants and anti-anxiety medication. Another option to help relieve anxiety is getting exercise, trying meditation, and eating healthy. Click below to help destress and relieve anxiety on this website!

Depression

The following information belongs to the Americal Psychiatric Association. Please visit this link to get more information.

Depression occurs when one experiences prolonged intense feelings of sadness or lack of interest in once enjoyed activities. If you are experiencing any of these symptoms, you may be suffering from depression:

- Having a depressed mood, feeling worthless or guilty

- Changes in appetite

- Loss of energy, trouble sleeping

- Difficulty thinking, thoughts of death/suicide

You are not alone. About 17% of the population will experience depression at least once in their life.

Treatment: Typical treatment options for depression are psychotherapy and medication.

Academic Pressure

Most people face stress daily. Most of the stress comes from school. The stress varies with what level of schooling students are in varying from elementary school to college.


Academic Stress in Various Age Groups


Age Causes Symptoms
Children Being the center of attention, presentations, bullying Crying often, refusing to go to school, bedwetting
Preteens Excessive workload, busy schedule, financial struggles Ill temper, self- harm, substance abuse
Teens Parental pressure, keeping up with school, social acceptance Skipping meals or overeating, sadness/depression, cutting classes
Adults Lack of sleep, balancing responsibilities, homesickness (college) Sleep problems, social isolation, fluctuating weight

What Teachers can do:

Teachers can plan their lessons and assigned workload with the students' wellbeing in mind. Instead of dishing out massive amounts of assignments, they can make sure that the homework is minimal, but also good and useful practice. Teachers can take on a supportive attitude towards their students to alleviate nervousness and stress.

If students are visibly stressed about the course, teachers could help by adjusting their curriculum accordingly so it isn't as overwhelming rather than continuing on as it is.


What Parents can do:

Having goals and expectationsare a normal thing for parents to have, parents should prioritize their child's health as well and take care not to overwhelm them. Some things that the parents can do to help the child is ensure that they receive a healthy amount of sleep each night and be a good role model for their children. Unhealthy behaviors are typically learned ones.

Eating Disorders

Eating Disorders are serious conditions related to eating behaviors that negatively impact health, emotions, and the ability to function in important areas of life. These typically develop in teens and young adults, but it can develop at other ages. They are mainly associated with body image issues and symptoms vary according to the type of eating disorder.

Types of Eating Disorders

×

Anorexia Nervosa

This eating disorder is characterized by abnormal low weight or intense fear of gaining weight. People with anorexia try to control their weight and body shape by extreme measure. For example, vomiting after eating and excessive exercise.

Bulimia Nervosa

People who have Bulimia have frequent episodes of binge eating and purging. They also limit eating during the day which will lead to more binge eating and purging. Basically, people who have this eating disorder will eat a lot in a short amount of time and try to get rid of the gain calories in an unhealthy way. People who have Bulima is probably preoccupied with their weight, shape, and also judged them harshly by their physical flaws.

Binge eating disorder

People who binge eat eat a lot. People who has this eating disorder eat quickly or eat a lot even if they are not hungry or being uncomfortabely full. After binge eating, they will not purge, but be ashamed of their eating habits. Being ashamed will lead to them eating in secret.

Rumination Disorder

The person who has Rumination persistently and repeatedly regugitate food after eating, but this eating disorder is not caused by anxoeria or bulimia. Sometimes food is rechewed, reswallowed, or spit out. Some people who has this eating disorder will spit out the food or eat less to avoid vomiting the food. However, this will lead to malnutrient. This is common in infancy.

Avoidant/Restrictive food intake Disorder

People who has this eating disorder fail to meet their minimum daily nutrient because of three reasons. First, they are not interested in eating. Second, they avoid food with certain characteristics such as color or smell. For the third and the last reason, they avoid eating for fear of choking or for other consequences of eating. This eating disorder's consequences are significant weight loss or failure to gain weight in childhood.


When to get Help

You should be getting help if you or a loved one is showing these symptoms:

- Skipping meals or making excuses for not eating

- Eating much more food in a meal or snack than what is considered normal

- Excessive exercise

- Negative feelings such as guilt or shame about eating habits

- Eating in secret


GETTING HELP

When

The following information belongs comes from the National Drug Helpline. Please visit this link to get more information.

Please get help if you are experiencing any of the following:

- Confused or strange thinking

- Changes in sleeping patterns, eating habits, or behavior

- Difficulty controlling anger and emotions

- Isolation from family and friends

- Erratic energy levels

- Seeing or hearing things that may not be there

- Feelings of uncontrollable hopelessness or despair

- Thoughts of ending your life

- Using drugs or alcohol to deal with your problems


How

Mental illnesses are not much different from physical ones in this regard. You can get mental help by reaching out to a doctor and screening for mental illness. If you have been diagnosed with mental illness, please seek treatment and know that you're not alone in your experiences. You can also look for local support groups to help cope with your symptoms. A good idea would also be reading up on treatments for the illness you have.
