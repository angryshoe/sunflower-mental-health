GETTING HELP - Hotlines

The following information belongs to the National Drug Helpline. Please visit this link to get more information.

If you are in an emergency/crisis, do not call a hotline. Call 911.

Hotlines are open telephone lines set up for a specific purpose; for mental health hotlines, you may speak anonymously about any topic and get help from professionals.


Hotlines should be called if you or a loved one exhibits any of the following signs:

Confused or strange thinking

Changes in sleeping patterns, eating habits, or behavior

Difficulty controlling anger and emotions

Isolation from family and friends

Erratic energy levels

Seeing or hearing things that may not be there

Feelings of uncontrollable hopelessness or despair

Thoughts of ending your life

Using drugs or alcohol to deal with your problems

Anxiety

The following information belongs to the National Alliance on Mental Illness. Please visit this link to get more information.

Anxiety disorder occurs when intense feelings of distress or nervousness begin to interfere with normal everyday activites. The following a few types of anxiety disorders:

General Anxiety Disorder: "Chronic, exaggerated worry" that interferes with concentration in common activites.

Social Anxiety Disorder: Irrational fear of embarrassment when it comes to social situations, generally leading to difficulty in conversations and isolation.

Panic Disorder: Characterized by instense feelings of terror that result in palpitations, chest pain, or shortness of breath.

Phobias: Characterized by severe reactions to irrational fears.
