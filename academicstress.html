About

Most people face stress daily. Most of the stress comes from school. The stress varies with what level of schooling students are in varying from elementary school to college.


Stress that Elementary School Students have

The clauses of stress are:

Being the Center of Attention : Eating an unusual looking lunch or being tardy
Presenting anything in front of the class : Being Scared that they will mess up and that will be passed around the entire school as gossip
Physical Education : Afraid of being the last one to be picked for any team sport or being required to meet the same exercise standard as the entire class

Symptoms:

Crying often

refusing to go to school

bedwetting


Stress that Middle School Students have

The clauses of stress are:

Homework load : Having more homework per night than the homework an elementary school student has
Busy Schedule : Having a lot of extracurriculars makes finding downtime be difficult
Financial Worries : Kids who cannot have the hottest brand of clothings or phones feel left out

Symptoms:

Retreating to bathroom

Self- harm

substance abuse


Stress that High School Students have

The clauses of stress are:

Parental Pressure: Kids are pressured to have good grades and trying to establish their independence at the same time
Earning good grades : Kids are struggling to please their parents of getting good grades and getting into a good school when getting into the college in the future as classes get more difficult
Social Acceptance: Kids who don't have their own group of friends worry about making friends and avoiding bullying

Symptoms:

Skipping meals or overeating

Sadness/depression

cutting classes


Stress that College Students have

The clauses of stress are:

Fear of Missing Out: Mistakes are made when balancing social activities and academic responsibilies at the samea time
Lack of Sleep: It is easy of kids to miss sleep since they have so much completing for their time
Missing Family and Friends: It is very common for kids to have homesickness and loneliness since they are living in the college dorm.

Symptoms:

Sleep problems

Social Isolation

Fluctuating weight


What Teachers can do:

Planning to make sure that students don't have a lot of homework night after night or that students don't have a lot of tests on the same day

Taking time to organize so students won't be overwhelmed

Listen to students' issues, writing them down on the board, so they can be addressed will help students put them aside


What Parents can do:

Having a lot of goal-related to activities is good for college, but don't overwhelm the kids

Make sure that kids have enough sleep. For kids ages 6-12, they should have 9-12 hours per night while 8-10 hours are recommanded for teens.

Be a good role model and model good self care for the kids to follow


All the above information comes from Community for Accredited Online Schools.
